You are welcome to play an early stage serious video game prototype.

Here is how you play it:

  • You have a tile map that consists of hexes. On top of that map you can see forest and city regions that stand apart from each other.
  • The goal is to connect all such regions (forests with forests and cities with cities) in one network.
  • After you are done playing (and you won), please remember your score and fill in the questionnaire at the bottom part of the page.
  • For that you have access to various type of roads at the left part of the screen.
  • Be mindful of the number of tiles of a certain type that you can place.
  • You can click on the road icon to select it.
  • While a road tile is selected, you can hover your mouse over the place where you would like to place the road.
  • Then you rotate the road if necessary by using your mouse wheel.
  • Some roads you need to rotate properly before placing.
  • To place a tile you press the left mouse button (left click).
  • For every tile you place you get one point from your final score deducted.
  • When you connect regions - you get points. Every region has weight that is added to your final score once connected.

Connection rules:

  • Green roads can be connected with other green roads in all directions and are used to connect forest regions.
  • Tiles with a brown road on them are used to connect city regions. Roads are considered correctly only if the brown road forms a line and is not interrupted.
  • You also have access to universal tile that can connect everything with everything (first tile in the list) but you only have 2 of them for a map.

Known bugs:

  • No new connections can be formed when initially connected with wrong types of road.
  • Performance issues.
  • Some tiles are difficult to click.

Good luck playing!
